# Page Structure

> Section-by-section layout of the new page, top to bottom. Each section gets a job —
> if a section doesn't have a job, it doesn't ship.
>
> Mood: clean tech, spring, clarity, Apple. (Full aesthetic: [visual-direction.md](visual-direction.md))

## Page flow

1. **Hero** — full-viewport, interactive
2. **iPhone product demo** — iMessage conversation in a device frame
3. **Use cases & demos** — concrete scenarios; introduces call + email channels
4. **How it works** — 3-step clarity strip
5. **Trust** — short trust/safety strip
6. **Final CTA + footer**

The sequencing is a runway: demo convinces → use cases expand → how-it-works
simplifies → trust disarms → CTA closes.

---

## 1. Hero

**Job:** in 5 seconds the visitor knows what Gigler is and feels the polish.

- Full-page (full viewport) section
- **H1 centered** — the one-liner (see [copy.md](copy.md))
- Subheadline: one quiet supporting sentence
- **Primary CTA in the hero: join the waitlist**
- **Interactive element:** reactive mesh / generative glass element — the "alive"
  tech signal, on the light canvas

## 2. iPhone product demo

**Job:** show the product actually being used; prove "you text it like a person."

- Product demo rendered **inside an iPhone frame**, Apple-keynote polish
- **Format: animated iMessage conversation** (decided — blue bubbles are the
  product's identity; instantly legible to the everyman)
- Show **one complete task arc**: user asks in plain English → Gigler replies,
  does real work → returns the result with proof (confirmation, booking, etc.)
- Not a montage. One task, start to finish, satisfying.

## 3. Use cases & demos

**Job:** make "it handles real life" tangible without claiming "it does everything";
land the multi-channel story (text it, call it, email it — same assistant).

- A small set (3–5) of **vivid, concrete scenarios** — the specific beats the
  general (see vision.md messaging challenge)
- Each scenario gets a mini-demo moment, glass-card treatment
- At least one scenario shows a **call** and one shows **email**, so the page
  communicates: one assistant, every channel, it remembers you across all of them
- Candidate scenarios (draft — tune in copy.md):
  - "Remind me to call Mom on Sunday" → it does, then nudges at the right time
  - Booking a dinner reservation end-to-end
  - "Find me a flight to NYC under $300" → options → booked draft
  - Calling Gigler in the car to check on something you texted it earlier
  - Forwarding an email and saying "handle this"

## 4. How it works

**Job:** kill any remaining "but what IS it / what do I do" confusion.

- Three steps, almost insultingly simple:
  1. Save Gigler as a contact
  2. Text it like you'd text a person
  3. It gets it done — and checks with you before anything big
- One line each, small icons/visuals, no paragraph text

## 5. Trust

**Job:** remove the silent objection ("an AI in my texts? creepy?").

- Short strip, 3 plain-language points, e.g.:
  - It remembers you — you never re-explain yourself
  - It asks before anything big or irreversible
  - Your data is yours: see it, edit it, delete it
- No security jargon. No "enterprise-grade encryption" talk.

## 6. Final CTA + footer

**Job:** repeat the ask for the scrolled-to-the-bottom visitor; close warm.

- Final CTA section: restate the one-liner (or a short emotional close) + the
  same **waitlist** CTA as the hero
- Footer: minimal — logo, a few links (about, contact, legal), no sitemap sprawl.
  No pricing link — pricing is "coming soon" and appears nowhere on the page.

---

## Decided

- **Primary CTA: join the waitlist** (hero + final CTA, same action)
- **No pricing anywhere** — "coming soon"; no pricing page, section, or dollar amounts
- **All six sections ship**, including "How it works" and "Trust" (both kept small —
  together roughly one viewport). If one ever has to be cut, cut "How it works"
  first; nothing else on the page carries trust.
